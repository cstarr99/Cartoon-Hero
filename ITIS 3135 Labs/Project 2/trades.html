Buying low in trades refers to buying players have low value because they have been playing poorly. The reason why you would try to trade for these players is because you have reason to think that they will eventually get better and therefore you get a good player at great value.

Selling high is the opposite of buying low. Selling high is deciding which players on your team are at the best value they will be. You could trade them after a good game to get more value than what they will be for the rest of the year.

There are few different sections you should be aware of during the season. The beginning weeks 1-5 is the first section and usually can help your team the most if you play it right. Nobody has any trend or idea of who is good in the start, so you can use this time to trade for people who have one bad week or trade away someone who has a good week if you think it won't continue.

The middle of the season is probably week 6 until the playoffs in week 14. This part involves needing your bench to be deep because injuries and bye weeks will make your team thin. If your team is deep then you will be able to pick up wins. The playoffs are the most important part because you need to make sure your players are healthy. You need to win 3 weeks in a row usually so you should build your team to peak at the end of the year and be consistent.

There are a few types of trades, and usually its either one of the following two. The first kind of trade would be an even trade in terms of the number of players meaning the players all equal to each other somehow. The other is trading multiple players for just one person.

This is the kind of trade I reccommend more. While it may seem like you are losing players, it is actually way better to have quality over quantity. Also, it gives you more room on your bench to add players.
